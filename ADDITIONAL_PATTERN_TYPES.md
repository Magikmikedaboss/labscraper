# Additional Pattern Types in Research (Beyond the Core 5)

## The Core 5 (What We Defined)

These 5 cover the full research lifecycle:

1. 🟢 **Convergence** - Clustering around same approach
2. 🔵 **Escalation** - Moving to higher validation
3. 🟠 **Stagnation** - Stuck on same problem
4. 🔴 **Abandonment** - Declining interest
5. 🟣 **Fragmentation** - No consensus on methods

**These 5 are sufficient for most analysis.**

But there are other patterns that appear in research...

---

## Additional Pattern Types (Optional Extensions)

### 🔷 6. Diversification Pattern

**What it means:**  
Research branches into new directions after initial convergence.

**Signals:**
- Started with one method, now using many
- Started with one entity, now testing many variants
- Increasing methodological diversity over time
- New entities being introduced

**Example:**
- 2020: Everyone using LC-MS
- 2021: LC-MS + HPLC + NMR
- 2022: LC-MS + HPLC + NMR + SPR + BLI
- Researchers are exploring alternatives

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research is diversifying into multiple methodological approaches."

**Why it matters:**  
Diversification can indicate:
- Limitations of original method being discovered
- Field maturing and exploring alternatives
- Preparation for different applications

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- Opposite of Convergence
- Can follow Stagnation (trying new approaches)
- Can precede new Convergence (exploration before focus)

---

### 🟤 7. Oscillation Pattern

**What it means:**  
Research interest cycles between high and low attention.

**Signals:**
- High activity → low activity → high activity
- Periodic spikes in publications
- Recurring interest after gaps
- "Rediscovery" language

**Example:**
- 2015: 20 papers on peptide X
- 2016-2018: 2 papers per year
- 2019: 18 papers on peptide X (renewed interest)
- 2020-2021: 3 papers per year
- 2022: 15 papers (another spike)

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research attention shows cyclical patterns of high and low activity."

**Why it matters:**
- Can indicate funding cycles
- Can indicate technological breakthroughs enabling new work
- Can indicate field responding to external events

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- Not Abandonment (interest returns)
- Not Convergence (not sustained)
- Temporal pattern overlaying others

---

### 🟡 8. Bifurcation Pattern

**What it means:**  
Research splits into two distinct camps or approaches.

**Signals:**
- Two dominant methods emerge
- Two competing theories
- Two different entity classes being studied
- Clear division in approaches

**Example:**
- Camp A: Using synthetic peptides
- Camp B: Using natural peptides
- Both active, both publishing, minimal crossover
- No consensus on which is better

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research has bifurcated into two distinct methodological approaches."

**Why it matters:**
- Can indicate fundamental disagreement
- Can indicate different applications
- Can precede eventual convergence on one approach

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- More structured than Fragmentation (2 camps, not many)
- Can follow Fragmentation (chaos → two options)
- Can precede Convergence (two → one)

---

### ⚫ 9. Plateau Pattern

**What it means:**  
Research reaches a stable state with consistent but not growing activity.

**Signals:**
- Stable publication rate
- Same methods being used
- Same entities being studied
- No major breakthroughs
- No major problems
- Just... steady

**Example:**
- 2018: 10 papers
- 2019: 11 papers
- 2020: 10 papers
- 2021: 12 papers
- 2022: 10 papers
- Consistent, not growing, not declining

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research has reached a stable plateau with consistent activity."

**Why it matters:**
- Can indicate mature field
- Can indicate niche application
- Can indicate "good enough" solutions

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- Different from Stagnation (not stuck, just stable)
- Different from Convergence (not increasing)
- Different from Abandonment (not declining)

---

### 🔶 10. Breakthrough Pattern

**What it means:**  
Sudden spike in activity following a major discovery or publication.

**Signals:**
- Sharp increase in publications
- Many papers citing same breakthrough paper
- Rapid adoption of new method/entity
- "Following X's discovery" language

**Example:**
- 2019: 5 papers on CRISPR peptides
- 2020: 8 papers
- 2021: 45 papers (breakthrough paper published)
- 2022: 60 papers
- 2023: 55 papers

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research shows a breakthrough pattern following a major discovery."

**Why it matters:**
- Indicates paradigm shift
- Indicates new capability unlocked
- Indicates field responding to opportunity

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- Can trigger Convergence (everyone adopts breakthrough)
- Can trigger Escalation (breakthrough enables new tests)
- Can end Stagnation (breakthrough solves problem)

---

### 🟠 11. Translation Pattern

**What it means:**  
Research moving from basic science to applied/clinical contexts.

**Signals:**
- Language shifts from "mechanism" to "application"
- Shift from academic to industry authors
- Shift from "understanding" to "treating"
- Clinical trial language appears

**Example:**
- 2018: "Understanding peptide folding mechanisms"
- 2019: "Peptide stability in biological systems"
- 2020: "Therapeutic peptide design"
- 2021: "Phase I clinical trial of peptide X"
- 2022: "Patient outcomes with peptide therapy"

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research shows translation from basic science to clinical application."

**Why it matters:**
- Indicates commercial potential
- Indicates regulatory pathway engagement
- Indicates real-world testing

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- Specific type of Escalation (academic → clinical)
- Can follow Convergence (consensus → application)
- Can trigger new Fragmentation (clinical challenges)

---

### ⚪ 12. Methodological Innovation Pattern

**What it means:**  
New methods being developed specifically for this research area.

**Signals:**
- "Novel method" language
- "We developed a new assay" language
- New techniques being published
- Methods papers increasing

**Example:**
- 2019: Using standard LC-MS
- 2020: "Novel LC-MS method for peptide X"
- 2021: "Improved assay for peptide stability"
- 2022: "High-throughput screening method"
- New tools being created

**Honest interpretation:**  
"Research shows methodological innovation with new tools being developed."

**Why it matters:**
- Indicates field maturity
- Indicates existing methods insufficient
- Indicates investment in infrastructure

**Relationship to Core 5:**
- Can follow Stagnation (need new tools)
- Can trigger Escalation (new tools enable new tests)
- Can trigger Convergence (new method becomes standard)

---

## Why We Chose the Core 5

The Core 5 pattern types we defined are:

1. **Sufficient** - Cover the full research lifecycle
2. **Distinct** - No overlap between types
3. **Observable** - Can be detected from your current data
4. **Actionable** - Lead to clear interpretations
5. **Honest** - Describe behavior without overclaiming

**The additional 7 patterns are:**
- More specific
- Harder to detect automatically
- Often combinations of the Core 5
- Useful for manual analysis but not essential for automation

---

## How Additional Patterns Relate to Core 5

| Additional Pattern | Core 5 Equivalent | Difference |
|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Diversification** | Inverse Convergence | Explicitly branching out |
| **Oscillation** | Abandonment + Convergence cycles | Temporal cycling |
| **Bifurcation** | Fragmentation with structure | Two camps, not many |
| **Plateau** | Stable Convergence | Emphasis on stability |
| **Breakthrough** | Rapid Convergence | Emphasis on trigger event |
| **Translation** | Escalation to clinical | Specific type of escalation |
| **Methodological Innovation** | Diversification of methods | Focus on tool development |

**Most can be described using combinations of the Core 5.**

---

## Should We Add Any of These?

### My Recommendation: **No, keep the Core 5**

**Reasons:**

1. **Simplicity** - 5 is manageable, 12 is overwhelming
2. **Coverage** - Core 5 already cover the full lifecycle
3. **Detectability** - Core 5 can be detected from your current data
4. **Combinations** - Additional patterns are often combinations of Core 5

**However, if you want to add ONE more:**

### **Best Candidate: Diversification**

**Why:**
- Clearly distinct from Core 5
- Observable in your data (increasing method diversity)
- Useful signal (indicates exploration after convergence)
- Complements Convergence (opposite trajectory)

**If we add Diversification, the 6 would be:**

1. 🟢 Convergence - Clustering
2. 🔷 Diversification - Branching
3. 🔵 Escalation - Increasing validation
4. 🟠 Stagnation - Stuck
5. 🔴 Abandonment - Declining
6. 🟣 Fragmentation - No consensus

**This covers:**
- Focus (Convergence)
- Exploration (Diversification, Fragmentation)
- Progress (Escalation)
- Challenges (Stagnation)
- Decline (Abandonment)

## My Final Recommendation

**Stick with the Core 5 for now.**

**Reasons:**
1. They're sufficient for your current goal
2. They're detectable from your current data
3. They're simple enough to explain
4. They're distinct enough to be useful

**If you later find you need more nuance:**
- Add Diversification as #6
- Or use combinations of Core 5 to describe complex patterns

**But don't add all 12. That's overcomplicated.**
